INDIAN LABOR LAWS — KEY PROVISIONS FOR WOMEN'S RIGHTS
=======================================================

1. SEXUAL HARASSMENT OF WOMEN AT WORKPLACE (PREVENTION, PROHIBITION AND REDRESSAL) ACT, 2013 (POSH Act)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Applies to all workplaces including private, public, organized and unorganized sectors.
- Defines sexual harassment as: unwelcome physical contact, demand or request for sexual favors, making sexually colored remarks, showing pornography, or any other unwelcome physical, verbal or non-verbal conduct of sexual nature.
- Every employer with 10 or more employees must constitute an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC).
- District Officers must constitute a Local Complaints Committee (LCC) for establishments with fewer than 10 workers.
- Aggrieved woman can file a complaint within 3 months of the incident (extendable by 3 more months).
- Complaint process: Written complaint to ICC → ICC inquiry within 90 days → Report to employer within 10 days of inquiry → Action within 60 days.
- Interim relief: Aggrieved woman can request transfer, leave up to 3 months, or other reliefs during inquiry.
- Penalty for non-compliance: Fine up to Rs. 50,000; repeated violations lead to cancellation of business license.
- Confidentiality must be maintained throughout the process.
- False complaints with malicious intent are also penalized (but mere inability to prove doesn't constitute false complaint).

2. MATERNITY BENEFIT ACT, 1961 (AMENDED 2017)
-----------------------------------------------
- Applies to establishments with 10 or more employees.
- Maternity leave entitlement: 26 weeks for first two children; 12 weeks for third child onwards.
- Adoptive mothers and commissioning mothers entitled to 12 weeks of maternity leave for children below 3 months.
- Work from home option: Employer may allow work from home after maternity leave period, based on nature of work.
- Creche facility: Mandatory for establishments with 50+ employees; mother entitled to 4 visits per day.
- No dismissal or discharge during maternity leave period.
- Penalty for employer violation: Up to 1 year imprisonment and/or fine up to Rs. 5,000.
- Bonus and other allowances not to be reduced during maternity leave.

3. EQUAL REMUNERATION ACT, 1976
---------------------------------
- Prohibits discrimination in wages between men and women for same work or work of similar nature.
- No employer shall pay less remuneration to a woman worker than that paid to a man.
- Prohibition on discrimination in recruitment, promotion, training, and transfer.
- Penalty for violation: Minimum Rs. 10,000, maximum Rs. 20,000 fine; imprisonment up to 1 month for repeat offenses.
- Workers can file complaint with Labour Commissioner.

4. FACTORIES ACT, 1948 — PROVISIONS FOR WOMEN
-----------------------------------------------
- No women shall be required to work between 7 PM and 6 AM (night shift restrictions).
- Separate toilet and washing facilities must be provided for women workers.
- Creche facilities mandatory in factories with 30+ women workers.
- Women must not be employed near cotton openers, or in certain hazardous operations.
- State governments can allow night shift work for women with adequate safety measures.

5. MINIMUM WAGES ACT, 1948
----------------------------
- Equal minimum wages for men and women in scheduled employments.
- Employer must display minimum wage rates at the workplace.
- Workers can file complaint with Labour Inspector for non-payment.
- Penalty: Fine up to Rs. 500 per offense.

6. EMPLOYEES PROVIDENT FUND (EPF) ACT, 1952
---------------------------------------------
- Mandatory for establishments with 20+ employees.
- Employer contribution: 12% of basic salary + DA.
- Employee contribution: 12% of basic salary + DA.
- New women employees (first-time EPF members) can contribute only 8% for first 3 years to increase take-home pay.
- Nomination rights: Women can nominate any family member.
- Can withdraw EPF for marriage, housing, medical emergencies.

7. CODE ON WAGES, 2019
-----------------------
- Consolidates Payment of Wages Act, Minimum Wages Act, Payment of Bonus Act, Equal Remuneration Act.
- Ensures timely payment of wages (daily, weekly, fortnightly, or monthly).
- Prohibition on gender-based wage discrimination.
- Inspector-cum-Facilitator system for grievance redressal.

8. EMPLOYEES STATE INSURANCE (ESI) ACT, 1948
----------------------------------------------
- Provides medical, maternity, disability, and dependent benefits.
- Applicable to establishments with 10+ employees (20+ in some states).
- ESI benefits: Full medical care for employee and family, sickness benefit (70% of wages), maternity benefit, disablement benefit.
- Women workers get enhanced maternity benefits under ESI in addition to Maternity Benefit Act.

9. PROTECTION OF WOMEN FROM DOMESTIC VIOLENCE ACT, 2005
---------------------------------------------------------
- Protects women from physical, sexual, verbal, emotional, and economic abuse.
- Covers women in any domestic relationship (wife, partner, mother, sister, daughter).
- Reliefs: Protection orders, residence orders, monetary relief, custody orders, compensation orders.
- Magistrate can issue emergency protection order within 3 days of application.
- Protection Officers appointed in every district to assist aggrieved women.
- Respondent can be directed to pay monthly maintenance and bear medical expenses.

10. DOWRY PROHIBITION ACT, 1961
---------------------------------
- Giving or taking dowry is a punishable offense.
- Penalty: Imprisonment for minimum 5 years and fine of Rs. 15,000 or amount of dowry (whichever is higher).
- Demand for dowry is also punishable.
- Any advertisement for dowry is punishable.

11. INDIAN PENAL CODE (IPC) — WOMEN-SPECIFIC SECTIONS
-------------------------------------------------------
- Section 354: Assault or criminal force to outrage modesty — Imprisonment 1-5 years.
- Section 354A: Sexual harassment — Imprisonment up to 3 years.
- Section 354B: Disrobing a woman — Imprisonment 3-7 years.
- Section 354C: Voyeurism — Imprisonment 1-3 years (first offense), 3-7 years (repeat).
- Section 354D: Stalking — Imprisonment up to 3 years (first offense), up to 5 years (repeat).
- Section 376: Rape — Minimum 7 years, maximum life imprisonment.
- Section 498A: Cruelty by husband or relatives — Imprisonment up to 3 years.
- Section 509: Word, gesture intended to insult modesty — Imprisonment up to 3 years.

12. WORKPLACE GRIEVANCE ESCALATION PROCESS
--------------------------------------------
Step 1: Report to Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) or HR department.
Step 2: If no resolution, escalate to Local Complaints Committee (LCC) — District Officer.
Step 3: File complaint with Labour Commissioner / Labour Department.
Step 4: Approach Labour Court / Industrial Tribunal.
Step 5: High Court (Writ Petition under Article 226) or Supreme Court (Article 32).
Step 6: National Commission for Women (NCW) — can take suo motu cognizance.
Step 7: State Women's Commissions for state-level issues.
