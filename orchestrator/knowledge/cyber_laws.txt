DIGITAL SAFETY & CYBER LAWS FOR WOMEN IN INDIA
================================================

INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY ACT, 2000 (IT Act) — KEY SECTIONS
----------------------------------------------------------

Section 43 — Unauthorised Access to Computer Resources
- Penalty for unauthorised access, downloading, or extracting data from another's computer.
- Covers hacking into email accounts, social media, or personal devices.
- Civil remedy: compensation up to Rs 1 crore.

Section 43A — Compensation for Failure to Protect Data
- Applies to body corporates (companies, platforms) that negligently handle sensitive personal data.
- Victims can claim compensation if a platform fails to protect their data after a breach.

Section 66 — Computer Related Offences
- Imprisonment up to 3 years or fine up to Rs 5 lakh, or both.
- Applies to: dishonestly accessing computer resources, unauthorised downloads, data theft.

Section 66A (Struck Down) — Note
- Section 66A was struck down by the Supreme Court in Shreya Singhal v. Union of India (2015).
- Posting "offensive" messages online is no longer a criminal offence under this section.

Section 66C — Identity Theft
- Fraudulently using another person's electronic signature, password, or unique identification feature.
- Punishment: Imprisonment up to 3 years AND fine up to Rs 1 lakh.
- Covers: stealing login credentials, impersonating someone on social media, using someone's OTP/UPI.

Section 66D — Cheating by Personation Using Computer Resource
- Using a computer resource to cheat by impersonating another person.
- Punishment: Imprisonment up to 3 years AND fine up to Rs 1 lakh.
- Covers: fake profiles, romance scams, impersonation for financial fraud.

Section 66E — Violation of Privacy
- Intentional capture, publishing, or transmitting the image of a person's private area without consent.
- Punishment: Imprisonment up to 3 years OR fine up to Rs 2 lakh.
- Covers: voyeurism, upskirting, recording without consent in private spaces.

Section 66F — Cyber Terrorism
- Acts intended to threaten the unity, integrity, security, or sovereignty of India using computer resources.
- Punishment: Imprisonment for life.

Section 67 — Publishing Obscene Material in Electronic Form
- Publishing or transmitting obscene material electronically.
- First conviction: Imprisonment up to 3 years AND fine up to Rs 5 lakh.
- Subsequent conviction: Imprisonment up to 5 years AND fine up to Rs 10 lakh.
- Applies to: sending explicit messages, sharing obscene images via WhatsApp/social media.

Section 67A — Publishing Sexually Explicit Material
- Publishing or transmitting sexually explicit material involving adults (consensual acts depicted).
- First conviction: Imprisonment up to 5 years AND fine up to Rs 10 lakh.
- Subsequent conviction: Imprisonment up to 7 years AND fine up to Rs 10 lakh.

Section 67B — Child Pornography / CSAM
- Publishing, transmitting, or browsing material that sexually exploits children.
- First conviction: Imprisonment up to 5 years AND fine up to Rs 10 lakh.
- Subsequent: Imprisonment up to 7 years AND fine up to Rs 10 lakh.

Section 72 — Breach of Confidentiality and Privacy
- Disclosing information of another person without consent while being in a position of trust or authority.
- Punishment: Imprisonment up to 2 years OR fine up to Rs 1 lakh.

Section 72A — Disclosure of Information in Breach of Lawful Contract
- Unauthorized disclosure of personal information in breach of a contract.
- Punishment: Imprisonment up to 3 years OR fine up to Rs 5 lakh.


INDIAN PENAL CODE (IPC) / BHARATIYA NYAYA SANHITA (BNS) — CYBER OFFENCES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------

Section 354C IPC / Section 77 BNS — Voyeurism
- Watching or capturing image of a woman in private act without consent.
- First conviction: Imprisonment 1–3 years + fine.
- Subsequent: Imprisonment 3–7 years + fine.

Section 354D IPC / Section 78 BNS — Stalking
- Following a woman or monitoring online activities despite clear disinterest.
- First conviction: Imprisonment up to 3 years + fine.
- Subsequent: Imprisonment up to 5 years + fine.
- Cyber stalking: repeatedly following on social media, tracking location, sending persistent unwanted messages.

Section 499 & 500 IPC / Section 356 BNS — Defamation
- Making false imputations to damage reputation via electronic means.
- Imprisonment up to 2 years OR fine.
- Cyber defamation: fake posts, screenshots with fabricated captions, false reviews.

Section 503 IPC / Section 351 BNS — Criminal Intimidation
- Threatening a person with harm to cause fear or force them to do/not do something.
- Covers: death threats via WhatsApp, threatening messages on social media.

Section 506 IPC / Section 351 BNS — Punishment for Criminal Intimidation
- Imprisonment up to 2 years, or fine, or both.
- If threat is of causing death or grievous hurt: Imprisonment up to 7 years.

Section 507 IPC — Criminal Intimidation by Anonymous Communication
- Sending anonymous threats (unidentified sender).
- Imprisonment up to 2 years.

Section 509 IPC / Section 79 BNS — Word, Gesture, Act Intended to Insult Modesty of Women
- Uttering any word, making sound, gesture, or electronic object to intrude upon privacy of a woman.
- Imprisonment up to 3 years AND fine.
- Covers: obscene messages, sexual harassment via electronic means.


NON-CONSENSUAL INTIMATE IMAGE (NCII) / REVENGE PORN
----------------------------------------------------

Legal Framework:
- Section 66E IT Act — primary provision (violation of privacy by capturing private images)
- Section 67A IT Act — publishing sexually explicit material
- Section 354C IPC — voyeurism
- Section 500 IPC — defamation
- Section 506 IPC — criminal intimidation (if used to threaten)

How to Report NCII:
1. Cybercrime Portal: www.cybercrime.gov.in — File complaint under "Report Cyber Crime"
2. Platform Takedown: Use platform's "Report" feature; reference Section 67/67A IT Act in report.
3. NCPCR Helpline: 1098 (if minors involved)
4. Police: File FIR at local police station or cyber cell.

Evidence Preservation for NCII:
- Take screenshots with timestamps.
- Note URLs, profile links, usernames.
- Do NOT delete original content before reporting — platforms need the link to remove it.


CYBERBULLYING AND ONLINE HARASSMENT
------------------------------------

Definition: Repeated harmful behavior directed at an individual through digital means.

Types:
1. Trolling — Deliberately provoking and upsetting someone with hostile messages.
2. Doxxing — Publishing private personal information (address, phone number) online without consent.
3. Impersonation — Creating fake profiles to defame or harass.
4. Spamming — Flooding someone with unwanted messages.
5. Pile-on harassment — Coordinated group attacks targeting one person.
6. Gaslighting — Manipulating someone into questioning their own reality via messages.

Legal Remedies for Cyberbullying:
- Section 66C (identity theft if impersonation involved)
- Section 66D (cheating by personation)
- Section 67 (if obscene content shared)
- Section 503/506 IPC (criminal intimidation if threats made)
- Section 499/500 IPC (defamation if false statements made)

Where to Report:
1. National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal: www.cybercrime.gov.in
2. Cyber Cell of local police
3. Women Helpline: 1091
4. iCall (TISS Helpline): 9152987821


ONLINE FINANCIAL FRAUD
-----------------------

Common Types:
1. UPI Fraud — Fraudulent payment requests via UPI apps (PhonePe, GPay, Paytm).
2. Phishing — Fake emails/messages posing as banks to steal credentials.
3. OTP Fraud — Tricking victim into sharing OTP via social engineering.
4. Matrimonial Fraud — Romance scams through matrimonial sites.
5. Job Fraud — Fake job offers requiring "registration fee" or "security deposit".
6. Investment Fraud — Ponzi schemes promoted via WhatsApp/Telegram groups.

Legal Provisions:
- Section 66C IT Act — Identity theft (fraudulently using someone's OTP, password).
- Section 66D IT Act — Cheating by personation using computer resource.
- Section 420 IPC — Cheating and dishonestly inducing delivery of property.
- Section 415 IPC — Cheating (obtaining consent by deceiving).

Where to Report:
1. Cybercrime Portal: www.cybercrime.gov.in
2. National Helpline 1930 (Cyber Financial Fraud — call within first hour for best chance of freezing money)
3. Bank's fraud helpline immediately if bank credentials compromised
4. RBI Ombudsman if bank is unresponsive


HARASSMENT SEVERITY FRAMEWORK
-------------------------------

Level 1 — Low Severity (Uncomfortable but not threatening):
- Unsolicited romantic messages (single instance)
- Mild offensive comments
- Unwanted friend requests/follows
- Recommended Action: Block, report to platform, document.

Level 2 — Moderate Severity (Pattern of harassment):
- Repeated unwanted messages after being blocked/ignored
- Defamatory posts (false statements)
- Impersonation accounts (fake profiles)
- Sharing private conversations without consent
- Recommended Action: Document all evidence, file complaint on cybercrime.gov.in, consider legal notice.

Level 3 — High Severity (Direct harm to safety):
- Explicit threats of violence or sexual assault
- Doxxing (publishing home address, phone number)
- Stalking (tracking physical movements)
- Non-consensual intimate image sharing / threats to share
- Recommended Action: IMMEDIATELY file police FIR, apply for protection order, contact cyber cell.

Level 4 — Critical Severity (Immediate danger):
- Credible death threats with personal information
- Active physical stalking combined with online threats
- Coordinated mob harassment with doxxing
- Sexual extortion (sextortion) with active demands
- Recommended Action: Contact police (100), apply for emergency protection order, preserve ALL evidence, consider temporary relocation of information.


ESCALATION PATHWAYS
--------------------

Step 1 — Immediate Safety:
- If in physical danger: Call 100 (Police) or 112 (Emergency Services)
- Women's Helpline: 1091
- Domestic Violence Helpline: 181

Step 2 — Evidence Preservation:
- Screenshot everything with timestamps
- Record URLs, usernames, profile links
- Save all messages (WhatsApp, email, DMs)
- Do not delete original content — it's evidence

Step 3 — Platform Reporting:
- Report to platform (Facebook, Instagram, Twitter/X, WhatsApp)
- Reference IT Act sections in detailed report for faster action
- Use platform's "Impersonation" or "Harassment" report categories

Step 4 — Formal Complaint:
- File complaint at: www.cybercrime.gov.in (available 24/7)
- Alternatively: Visit local Cyber Crime Cell
- For financial fraud: Call 1930 immediately

Step 5 — Legal Action:
- File FIR at police station (cognizable offences cannot be refused)
- Consult a cyber law attorney for civil remedies
- Seek protection order from court if stalking/threatening

Step 6 — Support Resources:
- iCall (Mental Health): 9152987821
- SNEHI Helpline: 044-24640050
- One Stop Centre: 181
- National Commission for Women: 7827170170


EVIDENCE COLLECTION GUIDELINES
--------------------------------

Digital Evidence Must Include:
1. Full screenshots showing sender information, timestamps, platform URL
2. Download/archive posts before they are deleted
3. Record IP-traceable information if available (email headers, etc.)
4. Screenshot your own profile showing you did NOT consent
5. Capture any usernames, display names, profile pictures
6. Save chat logs as PDF or export from app
7. For UPI fraud: Save transaction ID, phone number, UPI ID of sender

Chain of Custody:
- Do not alter screenshots (no cropping that removes metadata)
- Use original device to capture evidence where possible
- Email evidence to yourself to timestamp receipt
- Consider using a trusted third party to witness digital evidence

Admissibility Under IT Act:
- Electronic records are admissible under Section 65B of Indian Evidence Act
- Need Certificate from person in charge of computer (Section 65B certificate)
- Ensure hash values are preserved for integrity

PLATFORM-SPECIFIC TAKEDOWN PROCESS
-------------------------------------

Meta (Facebook/Instagram):
- Report > "It's inappropriate" > Choose category
- For intimate images: facebook.com/help/StopNCII
- Emergency: Use StopNCII.org (hashes image, prevents spread without showing it)

Google (Search/YouTube/Gmail):
- Search result removal: google.com/webmasters/tools/legal-removal-request
- YouTube: Report video > Choose appropriate violation
- Non-consensual intimate images: Report directly via support form

Twitter/X:
- Report tweet > Select violation type
- For doxxing: Specifically select "Sharing private information"
- For intimate images: Select "Intimate media"

WhatsApp:
- Long-press message > Report
- Block + report sender
- Forward evidence to WhatsApp at abuse@whatsapp.com

LinkedIn:
- Report profile/post > Harassment/inappropriate content
- Block connection and report

Telegram:
- For illegal content: Submit to @notoscam or report bot
- Block contact from profile settings


SEXTORTION — SPECIFIC GUIDANCE
---------------------------------

What is Sextortion:
- Threatening to release intimate images unless demands (money, more images, sexual favours) are met.
- One of the fastest-growing cybercrimes in India.

Immediate Steps if Experiencing Sextortion:
1. DO NOT pay — payment escalates demands, never ends.
2. DO NOT send more images — this confirms leverage.
3. Screenshot all threats and demands.
4. Block the person on all platforms.
5. Report to cybercrime.gov.in immediately.
6. Call 1930 if money was already sent.
7. Contact StopNCII.org to prevent image spread.

Legal Sections Applicable:
- Section 67A IT Act (sharing intimate content)
- Section 66E IT Act (violation of privacy)
- Section 384 IPC — Extortion (imprisonment up to 3 years)
- Section 385 IPC — Putting person in fear of injury to commit extortion
- Section 506 IPC — Criminal intimidation
